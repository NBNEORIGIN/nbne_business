1. WELCOME
----------
Welcome to the team. This handbook outlines the policies, procedures, and
expectations that apply to all staff members. Please read it carefully and
keep it for reference. If you have any questions, speak to your line manager.


2. EMPLOYMENT TERMS
-------------------
Your individual terms of employment are set out in your contract of
employment. This handbook supplements your contract and provides additional
guidance on workplace policies.

Notice Periods:
- During probation (first 3 months): 1 week
- After probation: 4 weeks
- Management roles: 8 weeks


3. WORKING HOURS & ATTENDANCE
-----------------------------
Standard working hours are set out in your contract and the staff rota.

- You must arrive at least 10 minutes before your shift starts
- If you are unable to attend work, notify your manager as early as possible
  and no later than 1 hour before your shift
- Persistent lateness or unauthorised absence may result in disciplinary action
- Shift swaps must be agreed with your manager in advance


4. DRESS CODE & APPEARANCE
--------------------------
All staff must maintain a professional appearance appropriate to their role.

- Uniform (if provided) must be worn during all shifts
- Hair must be clean and tidy; long hair tied back where required
- Minimal jewellery; no facial piercings in client-facing roles
- Closed-toe shoes must be worn at all times
- Name badges must be visible


5. HEALTH & SAFETY
------------------
The company is committed to providing a safe working environment in
accordance with the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974.

- Report all accidents, incidents, and near-misses immediately
- Follow all safety procedures and use protective equipment as required
- Keep work areas clean and free from hazards
- Fire exits must remain clear at all times
- Familiarise yourself with emergency evacuation procedures
- Do not operate equipment you have not been trained to use


6. DATA PROTECTION & CONFIDENTIALITY
-------------------------------------
We comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and
the Data Protection Act 2018.

- Client and business information is strictly confidential
- Do not share client details with unauthorised persons
- Personal data must only be accessed for legitimate business purposes
- Report any data breaches to your manager immediately
- Do not use personal devices to store client data


7. GRIEVANCE & DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES
---------------------------------------
If you have a concern or complaint about your employment:

Grievance:
1. Raise the matter informally with your line manager
2. If unresolved, submit a formal written grievance
3. A meeting will be arranged within 5 working days
4. You have the right to be accompanied at formal meetings

Disciplinary:
- Stage 1: Verbal warning (recorded)
- Stage 2: First written warning
- Stage 3: Final written warning
- Stage 4: Dismissal

Gross misconduct (theft, violence, harassment, intoxication at work) may
result in immediate dismissal without prior warnings.


8. LEAVE & ABSENCE
------------------
Annual Leave:
- Full-time staff: 28 days per year (including bank holidays)
- Part-time staff: pro-rata entitlement
- Leave requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance
- Peak periods may have restrictions on leave approval

Sick Leave:
- Notify your manager before your shift starts
- Self-certification for absences up to 7 days
- GP fit note required for absences over 7 days
- Return-to-work meetings will be conducted after all absences

Other Leave:
- Maternity/Paternity leave: as per statutory entitlement
- Compassionate leave: up to 5 days (manager discretion)
- Jury service: time off granted as required by law


9. TRAINING & DEVELOPMENT
--------------------------
We are committed to your professional development.

- Mandatory training must be completed within the specified timeframe
- Training records are maintained and reviewed regularly
- Speak to your manager about development opportunities
- Certifications must be kept up to date; expired certifications may
  affect your ability to carry out certain duties


10. CODE OF CONDUCT
-------------------
All staff are expected to:

- Treat colleagues, clients, and visitors with respect and courtesy
- Act honestly and with integrity
- Follow all company policies and procedures
- Maintain professional standards at all times
- Report any concerns about malpractice or wrongdoing
- Not engage in bullying, harassment, or discrimination
- Not use company resources for personal gain
- Represent the business positively at all times


ACKNOWLEDGEMENT
---------------
By continuing in your employment, you acknowledge that you have read,
understood, and agree to comply with the policies set out in this handbook.

This handbook is reviewed annually. The company reserves the right to
update policies as necessary. Staff will be notified of any changes.
