HEALTH AND SAFETY POLICY
========================

Version 1.0 | Effective Date: January 2026

POLICY STATEMENT
----------------
This company is committed to ensuring the health, safety, and welfare of
all employees, clients, visitors, and contractors so far as is reasonably
practicable, in accordance with the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
and all associated regulations.

Management will ensure that:
- Adequate resources are provided to implement this policy
- Risk assessments are conducted and reviewed regularly
- Safe systems of work are established and maintained
- Staff receive appropriate health and safety training
- The workplace is maintained in a safe condition
- This policy is reviewed annually and updated as necessary


RESPONSIBILITIES
----------------

Employer / Owner:
- Overall responsibility for health and safety
- Ensure adequate funding for safety measures
- Appoint competent persons to assist with H&S duties
- Review this policy annually

Managers:
- Implement this policy within their areas of responsibility
- Ensure risk assessments are completed and acted upon
- Report and investigate all accidents and incidents
- Ensure staff are trained and competent
- Conduct regular workplace inspections

All Staff:
- Take reasonable care of their own health and safety
- Take reasonable care not to endanger others
- Cooperate with management on health and safety matters
- Report hazards, accidents, and near-misses immediately
- Use equipment and substances in accordance with training
- Not interfere with or misuse safety equipment


RISK ASSESSMENT
---------------
Risk assessments will be carried out:
- For all work activities and processes
- When new equipment, substances, or procedures are introduced
- Following any accident or incident
- At regular intervals (minimum annually)

Risk assessments will identify:
1. Hazards present in the workplace
2. Who might be harmed and how
3. Existing control measures
4. Additional measures required
5. Review dates

All risk assessments are documented and accessible to relevant staff.


FIRE SAFETY
-----------
- Fire risk assessments are conducted annually
- Fire detection and alarm systems are tested weekly
- Fire extinguishers are inspected annually by a competent person
- Emergency evacuation drills are conducted every 6 months
- Fire exits and escape routes must remain clear at all times
- All staff must know the location of fire exits and assembly points
- Fire wardens are appointed and trained

In the event of fire:
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm
2. Call 999
3. Evacuate via the nearest safe exit
4. Assemble at the designated assembly point
5. Do not re-enter the building until authorised


FIRST AID
---------
- Adequate first aid equipment is provided and maintained
- Appointed first aiders are trained and certificates kept current
- First aid kits are checked monthly and restocked as needed
- All accidents and first aid treatment must be recorded in the
  accident book
- The accident book is kept in a secure, accessible location

First Aiders:
- Names and locations are displayed on the H&S noticeboard
- At least one trained first aider is present during operating hours


MANUAL HANDLING
---------------
- Manual handling risk assessments are completed for relevant tasks
- Staff are trained in safe lifting techniques
- Mechanical aids must be used where available
- Avoid manual handling where reasonably practicable
- Report any injuries from manual handling immediately


DISPLAY SCREEN EQUIPMENT (DSE)
------------------------------
- DSE assessments are carried out for regular users
- Workstations are set up ergonomically
- Regular breaks from screen work are encouraged
- Eye tests are available for DSE users upon request


CONTROL OF SUBSTANCES HAZARDOUS TO HEALTH (COSHH)
--------------------------------------------------
- COSHH assessments are completed for all hazardous substances
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are maintained and accessible
- Hazardous substances are stored securely and labelled correctly
- Appropriate PPE is provided and must be worn
- Staff are trained in the safe use of all substances they handle
- Spill procedures are in place and staff are trained accordingly


PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
------------------------------------
- PPE is provided free of charge where required
- Staff must use PPE as instructed
- Damaged or worn PPE must be reported and replaced immediately
- PPE must be stored correctly when not in use
- Training is provided on the correct use of all PPE


ELECTRICAL SAFETY
-----------------
- All portable electrical equipment is PAT tested annually
- Fixed electrical installations are inspected every 5 years
- Staff must not use damaged electrical equipment
- Only qualified electricians may carry out electrical work
- Extension leads must not be daisy-chained


WORKPLACE HYGIENE
-----------------
- Work areas must be kept clean and tidy
- Adequate welfare facilities are provided (toilets, washing, rest areas)
- Cleaning schedules are maintained and documented
- Food preparation areas comply with food hygiene regulations
- Waste is disposed of safely and regularly


ACCIDENT REPORTING
------------------
All accidents, incidents, and near-misses must be reported:

1. Provide immediate first aid if required
2. Report to your manager as soon as possible
3. Complete an entry in the accident book
4. Manager investigates and completes incident report
5. RIDDOR-reportable incidents are reported to the HSE

RIDDOR (Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences
Regulations 2013) reportable incidents include:
- Deaths
- Specified injuries (fractures, amputations, loss of sight, etc.)
- Over-7-day incapacitation
- Occupational diseases
- Dangerous occurrences


LONE WORKING
------------
- Lone working risk assessments are completed
- Communication procedures are in place for lone workers
- Check-in protocols must be followed
- Emergency procedures are understood by all lone workers
- Lone workers must have access to a charged mobile phone


STRESS AND MENTAL HEALTH
-------------------------
- The company recognises workplace stress as a health and safety issue
- Risk assessments include psychosocial hazards
- Support is available through line managers
- Confidential support services are signposted
- Workloads are monitored and managed


TRAINING
--------
All staff receive:
- Health and safety induction on their first day
- Role-specific safety training before commencing duties
- Refresher training at appropriate intervals
- Additional training when procedures or equipment change

Training records are maintained and reviewed regularly.
Mandatory training must be completed within specified timeframes.


CONSULTATION
------------
Staff are consulted on health and safety matters through:
- Team meetings
- Direct communication with managers
- Health and safety noticeboard
- Suggestion/concern reporting mechanisms


MONITORING AND REVIEW
---------------------
This policy is monitored through:
- Regular workplace inspections
- Accident and incident analysis
- Staff feedback
- External audits where appropriate

This policy will be reviewed annually or sooner if:
- There is a significant change in operations
- Following a serious accident or incident
- New legislation or guidance is published
