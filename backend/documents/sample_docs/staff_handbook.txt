1. WELCOME & INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Welcome to [Business Name]. We are delighted to have you as part of our team.

This handbook sets out the key policies, procedures, and expectations that
apply to all employees. It is designed to help you understand how we work
and what we expect from each other.

This handbook does not form part of your contract of employment but should
be read alongside your individual employment contract and any additional
policies that may be issued from time to time.

If you have any questions about anything in this handbook, please speak to
your line manager or the business owner.

================================================================================
2. EMPLOYMENT BASICS
================================================================================

2.1 Probationary Period
    All new employees will be subject to a probationary period of [3/6]
    months. During this time, your performance and suitability for the role
    will be assessed. Your notice period during probation is [1 week].

2.2 Job Description
    Your role and responsibilities are outlined in your individual job
    description, provided separately. This may be updated from time to time
    in consultation with you.

2.3 Changes to Terms
    Any changes to your terms and conditions of employment will be
    communicated to you in writing.

================================================================================
3. WORKING HOURS & ATTENDANCE
================================================================================

3.1 Standard Hours
    Your normal working hours are set out in your employment contract.
    You may be required to work additional hours from time to time to
    meet business needs.

3.2 Timekeeping
    You are expected to arrive on time for all scheduled shifts. Persistent
    lateness will be addressed under the disciplinary procedure.

3.3 Reporting Absence
    If you are unable to attend work, you must notify your manager as soon
    as possible and no later than [30 minutes] before your shift is due to
    start. You should provide:
    - The reason for your absence
    - Your expected return date
    - A contact number

3.4 Rotas & Shift Patterns
    Work rotas will be published at least [1 week] in advance. Shift swaps
    must be agreed with your manager before taking effect.

================================================================================
4. LEAVE & ABSENCE
================================================================================

4.1 Annual Leave
    Full-time employees are entitled to [28] days paid annual leave per year
    (inclusive of bank holidays), pro-rated for part-time employees.

    Leave requests must be submitted at least [2 weeks] in advance and are
    subject to business needs and management approval.

4.2 Sickness Absence
    If you are absent due to illness for more than 7 consecutive days, you
    must provide a fit note from your GP.

    Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) will be paid in accordance with current
    government regulations. Any enhanced sick pay arrangements will be
    detailed in your employment contract.

4.3 Other Leave
    - Maternity/Paternity/Adoption Leave: In accordance with statutory
      entitlements and company policy.
    - Bereavement Leave: Reasonable paid leave will be granted.
    - Emergency Leave: Reasonable unpaid leave for dependants.
    - Jury Service: Time off as required by law.

================================================================================
5. PAY & BENEFITS
================================================================================

5.1 Payment
    You will be paid [weekly/monthly] by bank transfer on the [last working
    day of the month / specified day]. Your payslip will be provided
    electronically.

5.2 Deductions
    Statutory deductions (Income Tax, National Insurance) will be made
    automatically. Any other deductions will only be made with your written
    consent or as required by law.

5.3 Pension
    You will be automatically enrolled into our workplace pension scheme in
    accordance with the Pensions Act 2008. You have the right to opt out.

5.4 Tips & Gratuities
    [Where applicable] Tips are handled in accordance with the Employment
    (Allocation of Tips) Act 2023. Our tip distribution policy is available
    separately.

================================================================================
6. CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

All employees are expected to:

- Treat colleagues, clients, and visitors with respect and courtesy
- Maintain professional standards of behaviour at all times
- Follow all company policies and procedures
- Protect company property and use it responsibly
- Maintain the confidentiality of business information
- Report any concerns about safety, conduct, or wrongdoing
- Not attend work under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Dress appropriately for your role (see uniform/dress code policy)

Failure to adhere to these standards may result in disciplinary action.

================================================================================
7. HEALTH & SAFETY
================================================================================

We are committed to providing a safe working environment for all employees,
clients, and visitors. Key responsibilities:

    Your Responsibilities:
    - Follow all health and safety procedures
    - Report hazards, accidents, or near-misses immediately
    - Use equipment safely and as trained
    - Attend all required safety training
    - Maintain good housekeeping standards

    Our Responsibilities:
    - Provide a safe working environment
    - Conduct regular risk assessments
    - Provide appropriate training and equipment
    - Investigate accidents and incidents
    - Maintain relevant insurance and certifications

Our full Health & Safety Policy is available as a separate document.

================================================================================
8. DATA PROTECTION & CONFIDENTIALITY
================================================================================

8.1 Data Protection
    We comply with the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018. You must:
    - Only access personal data necessary for your role
    - Keep personal data secure and confidential
    - Report any data breaches immediately to management
    - Complete data protection training when required

8.2 Confidentiality
    During and after your employment, you must not disclose confidential
    business information, client details, financial data, or trade secrets
    to any unauthorised person.

================================================================================
9. DISCIPLINARY & GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES
================================================================================

9.1 Disciplinary Procedure
    We follow the ACAS Code of Practice on disciplinary procedures:

    Stage 1: Verbal Warning (recorded)
    Stage 2: First Written Warning
    Stage 3: Final Written Warning
    Stage 4: Dismissal

    Gross misconduct may result in summary dismissal without notice.
    Examples include theft, fraud, violence, serious health and safety
    breaches, and discrimination/harassment.

9.2 Grievance Procedure
    If you have a concern or complaint:
    1. Raise it informally with your line manager
    2. If unresolved, submit a formal written grievance
    3. A meeting will be held within [5 working days]
    4. You will receive a written response within [5 working days]
    5. You have the right to appeal

    You have the right to be accompanied by a colleague or trade union
    representative at any formal meeting.

================================================================================
10. EQUALITY, DIVERSITY & INCLUSION
================================================================================

We are committed to equality of opportunity and treating all people with
dignity and respect, regardless of:

- Age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership,
  pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, or sexual orientation

We will not tolerate discrimination, harassment, bullying, or victimisation
in any form. Any such behaviour will be treated as a serious disciplinary
matter.

If you experience or witness discrimination or harassment, please report
it to your manager or the business owner immediately.

================================================================================
11. SOCIAL MEDIA & COMMUNICATIONS
================================================================================

11.1 Personal Social Media
     You are free to use social media in your personal time but must not:
     - Post content that could damage the business reputation
     - Share confidential business or client information
     - Make defamatory comments about colleagues or the business
     - Use company logos or branding without permission

11.2 Business Social Media
     Only authorised staff may post on behalf of the business. All content
     must be approved before publication.

11.3 Company Communications
     Business communications (email, messaging) should be professional
     and appropriate at all times. These systems may be monitored.

================================================================================
12. TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT
================================================================================

12.1 Notice Period
     Notice periods are set out in your employment contract. During your
     notice period, you are expected to continue working to your usual
     standard and complete any handover tasks.

12.2 Return of Property
     On leaving, you must return all company property including keys,
     equipment, uniforms, and any documents or data.

12.3 References
     References will be provided on request and will confirm dates of
     employment and job title as a minimum.
