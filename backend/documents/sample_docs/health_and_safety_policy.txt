PART 1: GENERAL STATEMENT OF POLICY
================================================================================

[Business Name] Ltd is committed to ensuring, so far as is reasonably
practicable, the health, safety, and welfare of all employees, clients,
contractors, and visitors to our premises.

We recognise our legal duties under the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act
1974 and will take all reasonably practicable steps to meet them.

We will:

- Provide and maintain a safe working environment
- Provide adequate welfare facilities
- Ensure safe handling, storage, and use of substances
- Provide information, instruction, training, and supervision
- Maintain all equipment and systems of work in a safe condition
- Ensure safe access to and from the workplace
- Conduct and regularly review risk assessments
- Consult with employees on matters of health and safety
- Record and investigate accidents and incidents
- Review this policy at least annually

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and anyone affected
by our activities.

PART 2: ORGANISATION — ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

2.1 BUSINESS OWNER / DIRECTOR

    Overall responsibility for health and safety rests with the business
    owner/director. They will:

    - Ensure adequate resources are provided for health and safety
    - Appoint competent persons to assist with health and safety duties
    - Set health and safety objectives and monitor performance
    - Review this policy annually or after any significant change
    - Lead by example in promoting a positive safety culture

2.2 MANAGERS / SUPERVISORS

    Managers and supervisors are responsible for:

    - Implementing this policy in their areas of responsibility
    - Ensuring employees follow safe working procedures
    - Conducting risk assessments for tasks and activities
    - Reporting hazards, accidents, and near-misses
    - Ensuring new employees receive safety inductions
    - Maintaining records of training, inspections, and incidents
    - Ensuring first aid provisions are adequate and accessible

2.3 ALL EMPLOYEES

    Every employee has a legal duty to:

    - Take reasonable care of their own health and safety and that of
      others who may be affected by their actions
    - Co-operate with management on health and safety matters
    - Use equipment and substances in accordance with training
    - Report any hazards, defects, accidents, or near-misses immediately
    - Not interfere with or misuse anything provided for health and safety
    - Attend all required health and safety training
    - Follow all safe working procedures and use PPE as required

2.4 CONTRACTORS & VISITORS

    All contractors must comply with our health and safety requirements
    and provide evidence of their own insurance and risk assessments
    where applicable. Visitors must be signed in, briefed on emergency
    procedures, and accompanied where necessary.

PART 3: ARRANGEMENTS
================================================================================

3.1 RISK ASSESSMENTS

    We will conduct risk assessments for all significant hazards. Risk
    assessments will be:

    - Carried out by a competent person
    - Recorded in writing
    - Communicated to relevant employees
    - Reviewed at least annually or when circumstances change
    - Available for inspection by employees and enforcing authorities

    Key risk assessments include:
    - General workplace risk assessment
    - Fire risk assessment
    - COSHH assessments (substances hazardous to health)
    - Manual handling assessments
    - Display screen equipment (DSE) assessments
    - Young persons risk assessments (if applicable)
    - New and expectant mothers risk assessments (if applicable)

3.2 FIRE SAFETY

    In accordance with the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005:

    - A fire risk assessment has been carried out and is reviewed annually
    - Fire detection and alarm systems are tested weekly
    - Emergency lighting is tested monthly
    - Fire extinguishers are inspected annually by a competent person
    - Fire evacuation drills are conducted at least twice per year
    - Fire exits and escape routes are kept clear at all times
    - All employees receive fire safety training on induction and annually

    FIRE EVACUATION PROCEDURE:
    1. On hearing the fire alarm, stop work immediately
    2. Leave the building by the nearest safe exit
    3. Do NOT stop to collect personal belongings
    4. Do NOT use lifts
    5. Go to the designated assembly point: [LOCATION]
    6. Report to the fire warden / person in charge
    7. Do NOT re-enter the building until authorised to do so

    Fire Warden(s): [Name(s)]
    Assembly Point:  [Location]

3.3 FIRST AID

    In accordance with the Health and Safety (First-Aid) Regulations 1981:

    - A first aid needs assessment has been carried out
    - First aid boxes are located at: [LOCATION(S)]
    - First aid boxes are checked and restocked regularly
    - Appointed First Aider(s): [Name(s)] — Certificate expires: [Date]

    In case of serious injury or illness:
    1. Call 999 immediately
    2. Administer first aid if trained and safe to do so
    3. Do NOT move the casualty unless in immediate danger
    4. Record the incident in the Accident Book

3.4 ACCIDENT & INCIDENT REPORTING

    ALL accidents, injuries, incidents, and near-misses must be reported
    immediately to a manager and recorded in the Accident Book.

    RIDDOR (Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences
    Regulations 2013) — The following must be reported to the HSE:

    - Deaths
    - Specified injuries (fractures, amputations, loss of sight, etc.)
    - Over-7-day incapacitation
    - Non-fatal injuries to non-workers requiring hospital treatment
    - Dangerous occurrences
    - Certain occupational diseases

    RIDDOR reports are made online at: www.hse.gov.uk/riddor

3.5 SUBSTANCES HAZARDOUS TO HEALTH (COSHH)

    In accordance with the COSHH Regulations 2002:

    - COSHH assessments are carried out for all hazardous substances used
    - Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are obtained and filed for all products
    - Employees are trained in the safe use of substances
    - Suitable PPE is provided and used
    - Substances are stored safely and labelled correctly
    - Spill procedures are in place

    Products used at [Business Name] include:
    [List key chemicals/products, e.g. cleaning products, hair dyes,
    sanitisers, etc.]

3.6 MANUAL HANDLING

    In accordance with the Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992:

    - Manual handling operations are avoided where reasonably practicable
    - Risk assessments are carried out for unavoidable manual handling
    - Employees are trained in safe manual handling techniques
    - Mechanical aids are provided where appropriate

3.7 EQUIPMENT & MAINTENANCE

    - All equipment is maintained in safe working order
    - Electrical equipment is PAT tested regularly
    - Fixed electrical installations are inspected (EICR) every 5 years
    - Gas appliances are serviced annually (Gas Safe registered engineer)
    - Equipment defects must be reported immediately and the item taken
      out of service until repaired or replaced

3.8 WORKPLACE ENVIRONMENT

    We will maintain:

    - Adequate temperature (minimum 16°C for sedentary work, 13°C for
      physical work)
    - Adequate ventilation and lighting
    - Clean and tidy work areas
    - Suitable toilet and washing facilities
    - Access to drinking water
    - Adequate rest areas and break facilities
    - Safe floors, walkways, and work surfaces
    - Good housekeeping standards

3.9 PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)

    Where risk assessments identify the need for PPE:

    - Suitable PPE will be provided free of charge
    - Training will be given in its correct use
    - PPE must be worn as instructed
    - Damaged or worn PPE must be reported and replaced
    - PPE is stored correctly when not in use

3.10 TRAINING

    We will provide:

    - Health and safety induction for all new employees
    - Fire safety training for all employees (annually)
    - First aid training for designated first aiders
    - COSHH training for employees using hazardous substances
    - Manual handling training where required
    - Role-specific safety training as needed
    - Refresher training at appropriate intervals

    All training will be recorded and records maintained.

3.11 CONSULTATION WITH EMPLOYEES

    We will consult with employees on health and safety matters including:

    - Changes that may affect their health and safety
    - Arrangements for competent health and safety assistance
    - Information on risks and preventive measures
    - Planning and organisation of health and safety training
    - Health and safety consequences of introducing new technology

3.12 LONE WORKING

    Where employees work alone, a lone working risk assessment will be
    carried out. Lone workers must:

    - Have a means of communication
    - Follow agreed check-in procedures
    - Not undertake high-risk tasks alone
    - Know how to summon help in an emergency

3.13 STRESS & WELLBEING

    We recognise that workplace stress can affect health and will:

    - Monitor workload and working hours
    - Provide support for employees experiencing stress
    - Encourage open communication about wellbeing
    - Signpost to external support services where appropriate
